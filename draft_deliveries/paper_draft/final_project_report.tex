\title{Comparison of Quantum Krylov and UCCSD for Molecular Ground-State Approximation via Subspace Projection}

\author{Jihyeon Park}
\email{park687@wisc.edu}

%\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
This work compares Quantum Krylov and UCCSD as non-variational basis generators for
molecular ground-state approximation in a projected-subspace framework. Rather
than optimizing one ansatz, I compare how well each basis family generates a
useful low-energy subspace. Across the benchmark set, Quantum Krylov reaches chemical accuracy with
substantially fewer basis states than the non-variational UCC constructions,
showing a stronger expressivity-per-basis trend as system size grows. At the
same time, per-state resource estimates show that Quantum Krylov states are much more
expensive to prepare because they rely on Trotterized Hamiltonian evolution,
whereas selected UCCSD states remain shallow and inexpensive. The resulting
full-stack conclusion is a clear tradeoff: Quantum Krylov is favored at the algorithmic
layer by its smaller required subspace, while selected UCCSD is favored at the
logical and hardware layers by its lower circuit cost.
\end{abstract}
